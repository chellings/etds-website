What questions should you ask your team members before registration?
  • Name and surname
  • E-mail address
  • Student or non-student
    • Note that PhD students are seen as non-students this tournament
  • Sleeping sport or not
  • First time ETDS or not
  • 18+ at time of tournament
  • Diet/Allergies
  • Dancing:
    • Level:
      • Beginner (<1.5 year of dancing experience)
      • Breitensport
      • Open/Closed
    • Role
    • Mandatory blind dating
    • Partner for ETDS (if not mandatory blind dating)
  • Do you want to volunteer? (on top of your 1 obligatory helping hour)
  • Do you want to/can you volunteer as first aid?
  • Do you have an Emergency Response (BHV) license?
  • Do you want to/can you adjudicate?
Several of my team members were not selected last ETDS. Will they have a higher chance to get in now?
We have chosen to give a small preference to participants who were previously not selected for the ETDS. This does not mean, however, that they will be selected by default. For the participant to be eligible it is important that they sign up with the same email address as with which they signed up for the previous ETDS.
TC Checklist (and what you should tell your participants)
  • Before they register:
    • Send your members an awesome mail to make them even more enthusiastic
    • Inform the beginners of the beginners class, the perfect way to dance your first competition
    • Where to look for information
    • Who to contact for what information (mostly you)
    • How and before when to register, including the necessary information
    • Make sure you check all class and blind date regulations for this person
  • After a lottery round:
    • Inform them that they have or have not been selected
    • How and when to pay
      • Also make sure you actually get the money transferred to us, the organization
  • Before the ETDS:
    • Think about a theme outfit (and actually have them get one)
    • Think about a team presentation (and actually make one of max 30 seconds)
    • Assign all OHHs (Obligatory Helping Hours)
    • Get them to maybe volunteer a bit extra
    • Coordinate your travels as a team
    • Make sure everyone knows what to take (see packing list)
    • In general make sure your teamies have all the necessary information to enjoy the ETDS experience to the fullest!
  • In case of cancellation:
    • Say that it may take until after the ETDS for the refund to be processed
    • In case of a late cancellation or empty waiting list, make sure the participant knows they may not get a (full) refund
    • Always make sure to cancel the registration in the portal

What is so important about the beginners class?
It is truly a low level entry point for beginning dancers:
  • No worries about experienced couples racing past
  • The presenter will count the beat
  • An extra general look round to get a feel of how things work
  • A great audience, because everyone loves to cheer on their beginners
What does the team presentation entail?
Generally in an ETDS, teams are just called by city and the entire team walks on the floor. In our case, every team has 30 seconds to do whatever they like to get on the floor, but make it awesome and in theme! Music can and should be provided to the organization. The best 3 team presentations get extra points for the tanzmaus.
What should I know about the classes? â˜…
This ETDS will have three main classes:
  • Beginners
  • Breitensport
  • Open

Beginners are the dancers who dance for one and a half year or less and have no competition experience (including ETDS/NTDS). Because of the history with covid, we have decided to extend the cutoff for beginners to March 1st.

Both Breitensport and Open will have a qualification round. Subsequently, Breitensport will be divided into four classes: Amateurs, Professionals, Masters and Champions. Open will divided into two classes.

Please note that we will be stricter than past ETDSes on a dancer's actual placement in the open class.

For more information, check the classes page.
What should I know about the blind dating?
There are some rules regarding the blind dating which you can find here. Basically, there are two kinds of blind dating during the ETDS; mandatory and voluntary. For the voluntary blind daters, there are no rules; they can dance with whomever they like!

For the mandatory blind dating we trust in the knowledge of the team captain. While the blind-dating page will give more detailed information, Any pair of dancers who have actively trained together before, danced competitions, or done an xTDS since 2016 are not allowed to dance together. One important difference is that Open class blind daters are allowed to dance with someone from the same team, while for Breitensport dancers this is not allowed.

For a Breitensport dancer who is obligated to blind date in their usual role as lead/follow, they may choose to dance in the opposite role and have no restrictions to the blind dating (i.e. voluntary blind dating). We trust the team captains use this rule within considerable limits.
