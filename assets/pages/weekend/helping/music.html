Friday night music player

To whom do I need to report?

The person to whom you need to report is the Volunteer Manager. You will find them at the information desk.

From whom do I take directions?

Please only do as instructed by this information page or if one of the following people instructs you otherwise:
  • The presenters
  • Someone from the orga team

How do I know when the blind date rounds are?

You can find this in the competition live timetable as linked above. However, please pay close attention to the presenter in case there are changes.

What are important times for me?

The important times for you are:
  • The official welcome. You will play the official theme song to introduce the organizing committee. They will indicate the song to you.
  • The blind date rounds.

What do I do when there is no blind date round happening?

Please just play any music from the free dancing playlist. In case the presenter makes an announcement, please lower the volume temporarily before and while the presenter is speaking.

What do I do during the blind date rounds?

  • In rounds 1 and 2 of both standard and latin, you will play 3 dances each and then again 3 dances each.
  • In other words, for standard, it's SW TG QS SW TG QS.
  • In every song, after every 30s of dancing you lower the volume for about 5-10 seconds for dancers to find another partner.
  • For every dance you do 4 short sections of dancing.
  • This means 4x(30+7.5)s = 2m30s for each dance.
  • In total this leads to 15 minutes per round.

  • In rounds 3 of standard and latin, you will play only 3 dances but you will play 5 sections of music of each 30s, totalling a round of about 10 minutes.

Tournament music player

To whom do I need to report?

The person to whom you need to report is the Tournament Manager. This is Joanna Wytrzyszczewska at all times of the weekend. She will be found at the tournament desk.

From whom do I take directions?

Please only do as instructed by this information page or if one of the following people instructs you otherwise:
  • The tournament chairman or assistant chairman
  • The tournament manager
  • Someone from the orga team

How do I know how many heats there are?

You can find this in the competition live timetable as linked above. However, please pay close attention to the presenter and the tournament desk in case there are changes.

How long do I play?

  • During the qualification rounds (both breiten and open), as well as the general look of the beginners, you play for at most 1:15
  • During all other rounds (except for the finals), you play for around 1:20 - 1:25
  • During the finals, you play for 1:25 - 1:30

Do note that the timing only starts from the first danceable beat. (This includes an intro with rhythm, but not one without)
The tournament desk choose to deviate from these timings.

What do I do in between dances/heats/rounds/classes

In between dances or heats, the ideal scenario is:
  • While the song is playing, you prepare your favorite filler.
  • At the end of the time mentioned above, you fade out in about 3-5 seconds to 0 volume.
  • You immediately start and fade in your favorite filler at a very low volume while the presenter leads the couples on/off the floor or to a next spot.
  • BEFORE the presenter introduces the next dance, you fade out to silence.
  • Then the presenter will say "Here is your <insert dance>."
  • You start the next song.

The goal is to have minimal time of silence. Only right after the dance (for a split-second), and before starting a new dance (while the presenter is announcing it) should there be silence.

In between rounds/classes, the ideal scenario is:
  • The exact same as above with one difference:
  • After the last dance, instead of a filler, you play the theme song (specifically the edit that starts from the chorus).
