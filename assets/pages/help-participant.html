Participant help

So... You cannot see the forest through the trees eh? (Pun intended) You can find all the basic information you need to know before and during the weekend right here on this page. We'll assume you have been to an ETDS (or NTDS) before. If not, check out the newcomers page.

For all questions, make sure you check with your team captain first. If they don't know, ask them to ask us. That way, we can keep all information flowing through the correct channels. If they don't respond, however, and you're panicking, then of course you can send us an email.

Before the ETDS

How do I register?
Registration goes through the team captains. If you are on this website, you will likely know who your team captain is. Payment also goes through your team captain or your association. If you don't know or don't have one, please send us an email.
My activation mail is not arriving!
First try by going to https://fall.xtds.dance/activate/new/example@example.com. Where you replace example@example.com by your own email. If this does not work, and you have checked your spam, please send us an email and we'll look into it!
Is there a packing list?
We have a packing list! You can find it here!
Can I park at the venue?
Yes, there are plenty of free parking spaces available. Make sure you set your navigation to the parking lot, and not the address of the venue!

The address of the parking lot is: Van den Broekweg, Delft. See the front page for more information.
Which train station is better?
If possible, go to the Delft Campus station. It is a 15 minute walk from there to the venue. Arriving at the central Delft station is also possible, but you'll need a bus/taxi/car pickup or walk for 45 minutes.
Which bus should I take?
Arriving from Delft station, take bus 40 towards Rotterdam. You’ll need to leave the bus/tram at the stop: TU - Sport en Cultuur. You will arrive just in front of the tournament building.

Important: this bus does NOT run on weekends!
Uncommon or less common things at this ETDS you should know about!
  • The team presentation is an actual presentation! Check with your team captain for more information!
  • You do not need a plate, bowl, or cutlery. A cup, however, is preferred for your coffee and tea.
  • There are slightly different rules regarding the mandatory blind dating, so make sure you check them out.
  • Open class dancers will also have a qualification round. They will need to choose a blind date partner for this.
  • Evening visitors will not be able to enter unless they have signed up beforehand.
  • Smoking is forbidden anywhere on campus, both inside and outside.
  • Sleeping halls do not need to be emptied during the day. However, they will be locked during the tournament.
  • There are lockers available to store your valuable stuff. See below for more information.
  • Ordering food to the venue (such as pizza's) is not allowed
  • Getting food and drinks may work slightly different from what you're used to. See the food and drinks page.
  • Shoes are not allowed in the sleeping halls

At the ETDS - General

Is smoking allowed?
Smoking is forbidden anywhere on the university campus. This means even smoking outside is not allowed. If you do absolutely need a puff, you will have to go off campus (beyond the parking lot). Please do realize that at night this will not be possible

Failure to abide to this rule will result in immediate removal from the ETDS and possible fines may be incurred by the university!
So food is included? What about drinks? And what about dietary restrictions?
The food that will be provided is:
  • A snack on Friday
  • Breakfast and lunch on Saturday
  • Breakfast, lunch and dinner on Sunday
  • Breakfast on Monday


The exact menu for lunch and dinner will be on our website at least around one week before the start of the ETDS. During Breakfast and Lunch drinks like milk, coffee and tea will be provided.

There will be food provided for people with allergies. There is also special food for vegetarians and vegans. This has to be mentioned during registration! People who not mentioned to be vegan/vegetarian are not allowed to take this food. You don’t need to bring your own cutlery, bowl or plate!

You can buy extra drinks at the tournament hall or in the café that is located in the tournament venue. You can also buy extra food at the café. For more information on this see the food and drinks page.
If I want additional food/drinks, do I pay by card or by cash?
While most things are included in the price of the ETDS, all other payments have to be made by card. This is because TU Delft is a cashless university. We will not accept cash payments. Contactless payments are also possible. Credit cards are, however, NOT ACCEPTED. To be certain, make sure you have a Maestro or V-Pay card.
Can we order food to the venue?
No! this is sadly not allowed. If you want to order pizza on the Saturday night, you will have to order it and eat it outside of the venue. However, the X Café (at the venue) bakes some mean pizzas as well as many other tasty and affordable dishes. Their kitchen is open until 21:00. For snacks, the kitchen is open until 23:00.
Is there mandatory volunteering?
That is correct. Every participant is expected to help out to make the ETDS happen. Only in this way can we keep the costs low. Every team is assigned a number of OHHs (Obligatory Helping Hours). Division of these goes through the team captain.

Want to help out more? Great! You can enter this in the portal after logging in. If this doesn't work, or you don't know what to do, contact your team captain. Helping extra gives your team points to win the prize for the best overall team! For more information, see the volunteering page.
Will the sleeping halls stay open during the day/have to be cleared?
Good news, the sleeping halls do not have to be cleared during the day. You can just keep all your stuff in place. However, to avoid unnecessary guard shifts, the sleeping halls will be locked during the tournament. So make sure to take all the stuff you need when you leave in the morning.
Can I charge my phone at the venue?
There will not be a designated phone charging station. This means you have to bring your own extension cords for in the sleeping halls.
Can I store my valuables somewhere safe?
There are lockers available to store your valuable stuff. They are accessed with any RFID card, such as an OV card. However, these lockers will automatically open at 00:00h every night. So make sure to get your stuff out before that and lock it again after midnight.
What does the team presentation entail?
Generally in an ETDS, teams are just called by city and the entire team walks on the floor. In our case, every team has 30 seconds to do whatever they like to get on the floor. Music can be provided to the organization. The best 3 team presentations get extra points for the tanzmaus.
Can my friends/family come and watch?
Of course! They are more than welcome to come and watch you for the competitions. Simply have them pay for a visitor ticket at the entrance. Make sure to tell them the timetable, so they know when to come.

For the evening parties, visitors need to sign up and need to be with a dance association.

For more information see the visitor information page.
