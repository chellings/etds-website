Before the ETDS

So what actually is the ETDS?
The ETDS is a weekend filled with dancing, a tournament, dancing parties, socializing, shows and much more. It starts on Friday evening and ends on Monday morning. This means you will stay at the same venue for the entire weekend, where you will dance, eat and sleep. (With some exceptions) The whole weekend is themed. In this case the theme is "Festival of the Future". More on this later!
How do I register?
Registration goes through the team captains. If you are on this website, you will likely know who your team captain is. Payment also goes through your team captain or your association. If you don't know your team captain or don't have one yet, please send us an email.

At the ETDS - General

What does my weekend look like?
While some people choose to deviate from the general planning, for example arriving later or leaving earlier, the typical weekend looks like this:

You arrive on Friday. First you drop your stuff in the sleeping halls. You get changed (or not) and head to the tournament hall. This is where all the dancing takes place. There will be a snack waiting for you in the cafÃ©. This evening is all about blind dating (finding a partner). If you already have a partner, it's just a social dancing night!

On Saturday you will be dancing your standard tournament (unless you're an Open class dancer (more on classes below) in which case you dance latin). After your first round there will be a team presentation in which allteams introduce themselves. Breakfast and Lunch are included. Even if you are out of the tournament, it is a lot of fun to stay, watch and cheer on your team mates. After the tournament, it is customary to have dinner with your team or friends. Some will go into town or to the cafÃ© to have dinner and some will order food. (Eating ordered food inside is sadly not allowed) You have some time to freshen up before the evening will continue with the theme party. Everyone dresses up in a theme outfit. Often your team will have a certain take on the theme. Then a whole fun night of dancing ensues which continues until the last person goes to sleep!

Sunday is a repeat of Saturday with just a few differences. Firstly, you will be dancing latin (or standard if you are an Open class dancer). Secondly, dinner is included this evening. Lastly, rather than a theme party, there will be a gala night. Once again, the dancing will continue until the last person goes to sleep!

After waking up on Monday, you will start by packing up your stuff. We all meet again at breakfast where everybody says their goodbyes to their (new) friends. We ask everyone to stay a bit to help clearing up the big things. Then everyone leaves back to their respective cities with many great new memories.

If I want additional food/drinks, do I pay by card or by cash?
While most things are included in the price of the ETDS, all other payments have to be made by card. This is because TU Delft is a cashless university. We will not accept cash payments. Contactless payments are also possible. Credit cards are, however, NOT ACCEPTED. To be certain, make sure you have a Maestro or V-Pay card.
So where will I be sleeping?
Most people as sleeping in the two main sport halls that are designated for sleeping. These halls are litterally a hop, skip and a quickstep run away from the tournamen hall. Every team has their own area, so you are sure to have a spot with people you know. As such, you should take your own air mattress, pump, sleeping bag, etc.

Some people, however, choose to sleep elsewhere. For example at friends or in a hotel. This is your own choice, of course. Make sure that if you do change your mind about where you sleep, that you let your team captain know. This is because it is important for us to know how many people are sleeping at the venue.
And what about showers?
Long story short, we have plenty of showers and changing rooms reserved for you. Most are shared showers, but only with other dancers (of the same gender), so don't worry.
I heard that I have to help out too...?
That is correct. Every participant is expected to help out to make the ETDS happen. Only in this way can we keep the costs low. Every team is assigned a number of OHHs (Obligatory Helping Hours). Division of these goes through the team captain.

Want to help out more? Great! You can enter this in the portal after logging in. If this doesn't work, or you don't know what to do, contact your team captain. Helping extra gives your team points to win the prize for the best overall team! For more information, see the volunteering page.

At the ETDS - Dancing

What should I wear during the tournament?
At the ETDS, real competition clothing is not allowed. This normally means; no full dresses or tail suits, no feathers and stones etc. Most importantly, you should wear something you feel good in and can dance comfortably in. It should not restrict your movement.

For the ladies:
Wear a simple dress or skirt. I normally wear my trainings skirt. Take something that covers breasts, belly and buttocks. Wear short pants underneath your skirt if you are not sure everything is covered while moving ;). For the ladies heel protectors are required.

For the men:
Donâ€™t wear jackets! They have shoulder pads that look very ugly when keeping your arms up. Wearing a vest (waistcoat) and a button down shirt is recommended. Moreover, choose trousers in which you can move freely. Dancing shoes are preferred, however dress shoes are acceptable too. Just make sure not to wear sneakers.

For more details, check all restrictions on the dress code page!
What's this about classes? â˜…
During the ETDS there are different classes, so you always dance against people of your own level. This ETDS will have two main classes:
  • Breitensport
  • Open

Breitensport is the largest class, most dancers should be dancing in this class. Blind dating is obligatory for some of the Breitensport dancers. This depends on your level of competition dancing. The Breitensport starts with a qualification round. This is to determine your level. After this round everyone is placed in one or the four divisions; Amateurs, Professionals, Masters and Champions (in ascending level).

The Open class dancers are the ones who actively dance competitions at a higher level. Blind dating is obligatory for this class. The Open class starts with a qualification round as well.

For more information, see the classes page.
How do the rounds work?
After the first round, a number of couples are immediately placed through to the second round. The rest has to go again in a so-called redance. This gives the judges a second chance to take a closer look. Don't worry if you are in the redance, you still have all the chances of getting into the final!

From the redance, again a number of couples are selected for the second round. For the rest, the competition sadly ends. However, this means that everybody dances at least 3 rounds.

All couples for the second round will have to dance again. Every intermediate round, some couples are placed through and some are eliminated. This continues until 6 couples remain in the final. Do note that in later rounds you may have to dance more dances.

All couples that were in the semi-final are called to the entrance of the floor at the start of the final. Then one by one, the finalists are called onto the floor. Together they will dance the final round. After all finals, the prize presentation will start in which the finalists will hear the results.
Which dances do I have to dance?
The dances that you have to dance differ per class in which you are placed. Furthermore, this may mean you get additional dances in later rounds. You can find this information here.
And what if I don't have a partner?
No problem! On Friday night, the whole event revolves around finding everybody a partner for the weekend. Or two! There are many more like you, coming to the ETDS without a partner. Through the lottery system we have made sure that there are an equal number of leaders and followers.

Throughout the evening (and even some more on saturday night) there will be several rounds of speed-dating-dancing. You will have several 30-second dances with different dance partners. Once you find the partner of choice, you sign up together at the registration desk. You don't need the same partner for standard and latin, but it is allowed.

There are some rules regarding the blind dating which you can find here. Basically, there are two kinds of blind dating during the ETDS; mandatory and voluntary. For the voluntary blind daters, there are no rules; you can dance with whomever you like! If you are dancing competitions at a certain level, blind dating is mandatory for you. To find this out, check out those rules.

In general, don't be too worried. Your team captain and teamies are there to help you find that awesome dance partner.
So what is important during the competiton
Aaah, that's a difficult one. All judges have their specific things which they pay attention to. Of course they look at whether you are dancing in rhythm, do the correct technique, etc. However, they may also look at how you get on and off the floor. Or how you present yourself at the end of the dance.

Several tips from an experienced dancer to the beginners:
  • Don't think too hard about technique on the floor, then it's too late already
  • Your round starts the moment you walk onto the floor, not when the first music plays. Similarly, your round only ends the moment you walk off the floor, not after your presentation.
  • You are dancing for yourself and for the audience in the first place. Judges come second. So show you're having fun!
  • If everybody around is is clearly clapping in a different beat than in which you are dancing, you're likely out of rhythm. Stop, take a deep breath, and try again. There's no harm in that!
  • Most importantly: have fun!
